Student Support Bot

An AI-powered assistant embedded in the Student Hub. Answers common queries, guides students to the right place, and raises support cases when needed. Launched to all university students in March 2026.

In progress

Now

  • Bot live for 100% of university students since 28 March
  • Cases down 38% compared to the same period last year
  • Comms campaign running this week — email, explainer video, and in-app notification to all students
  • Two known issues with the bot being actively managed with Learnwise: an escalation loop, and a missing case button
  • Agent survey data analysed — results confirm the phone increase is explained by student awareness gaps and case-level demand, not bot failure

Next

  • Confirmed fix timelines from Learnwise for the two escalation issues
  • Validate that the recent visibility fix has brought the remaining students who couldn't see the bot into the experience
⚠ Fix ETAs from Learnwise not yet confirmed — chasing this week.

Later

  • Bot conversations visible to support agents in Salesforce, so agents have full context when a student escalates to a human
  • Milestones and case history surfaced for students who contact via channels other than the web bot

Academic Appeals & Extenuating Circumstances

Moving the student process for raising Academic Appeals and Extenuating Circumstances from a legacy system into Salesforce. Students will submit via the Hub; the ORC and Results & Awards teams will manage cases in Salesforce. ~10,000 cases per year.

In progress

Now

  • Core Salesforce case setup completed and passed quality review
  • Student-facing Hub forms in development — feature flag created, key screens in progress
  • How students submit evidence confirmed (email-based, consistent with existing process)
  • Decision made: all in-flight cases (~100) will move to Salesforce at go-live — no dual running

Next

  • Product review and sign-off of the Salesforce case setup
  • Confirm which team owns the notification emails sent to students when their case status changes
  • Start data migration planning — mapping fields from the old system to Salesforce
  • ORC and Results & Awards team review of case management screens
⚠ Data migration planning not yet started — this is the longest lead-time item and needs unblocking.

Later

  • Full go-live — students submit via Hub, ORC manages in Salesforce
  • Historical AA/EC records archived to long-term storage (6-year retention requirement)
  • Legacy system retired for this process
  • Two Salesforce reports live: one for caseworkers (with student details), one for trends (anonymised)

Legacy Attendance Decommission

Replacing the old QR-scan attendance tracking system with the new Tutor Portal. University student attendance is now recorded and reported through the new platform. The old system is being shut down.

On track

Now

  • New Tutor Portal fully live for university students — attendance data flowing correctly
  • Online Classroom auto-marking live since 29 April — system automatically records attendance for online sessions, removing the need for manual tutor marking
  • Old QR flow turned off
  • Attendance reports repointed to the new system

Next

  • Final removal of remaining old attendance infrastructure (in progress this sprint)
  • Resolve a data linking gap between Tutor Portal and timetabling data — needed for compliance teams to target specific student groups for audits

Later

  • Database decommission — separate decision, no active timeline yet
  • Potential extension of the auto-marking pipeline to additional learner types beyond university students
